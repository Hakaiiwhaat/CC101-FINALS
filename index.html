BEYOND EARTH

The smallest planet, Mercury, has a heavily cratered surface and extreme temperature variations due to its proximity to the Sun.

Venus, similar in size to Earth, has a thick, toxic atmosphere and experiences a runaway greenhouse effect, making it the hottest planet.

Earth, the only known planet with abundant liquid water and life, boasts diverse climates, landscapes, and a protective atmosphere.

Earth's only natural satellite is simply called "the Moon" because people didn't know other moons existed until Galileo Galilei discovered four moons orbiting Jupiter in 1610. In Latin, the Moon was called Luna, which is the main adjective for all things Moon-related: lunar.

Mars, the "Red Planet," features a rusty surface, polar ice caps, and has been a focus of exploration for potential signs of past life.

Jupiter, the largest planet, is a gas giant with a dynamic atmosphere, prominent bands, and a system of colorful storms, including the Great Red Spot.

Saturn, famous for its stunning ring system, is a gas giant with a diverse collection of moons and a prominent hexagonal storm at its north pole.

Uranus, an ice giant, is tilted on its side, and its atmosphere contains methane, giving it a bluish-green hue.

Neptune, another ice giant, features a dynamic atmosphere with the fastest winds in the solar system and a series of dark storms, such as the Great Dark Spot.

The Sun, a luminous ball of hot, ionized gas, is the center of our solar system, providing heat and light essential for the sustenance of life on Earth.
